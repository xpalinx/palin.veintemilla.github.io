Chocorun

Chocorun chocorun chocorrirrirun chocorun chocorun chocorrirrirun chocorun

This game was developed by me in one month for a university project for the communication faculty. This was the result of studying in the semester the principles of game narrative vs ludology. In this case I made a chocolate fox that runs from hungry villagers that want to eat him. This was my first approach to Unity. I was not rigorous with the design because I actually wanted to have something good at the end. I failed, learned a lot, but failed. We did not finish our prototype. I learned to build terrains, to manage collisions, to animate, to control movement with rigid bodies and capsule colliders, managed NavMesh basics, UI, etc...

Below, I am going to show you my progress. Enjoy!

Pepito

This was my first model. I did one animation but didn’t feel enough and I had no time so i tried something else. My teacher told me about Mixamo, so I used it, it did pretty well for the prototype.

AI

For the villagers I used mixamo animations as I tried to do some, but they were not enough. The animations came with model, so I used it. The other humanoid I model it myself and retargeted the animations. There are two animations for them, one to bite the fox, and the other one to hit other villagers.

Animator for humanoids/a>

This was my result for the animations of the villagers. I used the built-in function from unity Standard Asset for AI's code and studied unity’s animator.

Blooper

It was exhausting finding a dog... The image says: Here I would put a dog... If I had one! On this very first scene, the AI did not work, as the NavMesh needed a terrain. Now I know it wasn't necessary.

First idea

As I new a bit of mixamo y tried to apply an animation to this one. It is hard to find dogs with animations... Don't ask me why... Hopefully I found the base frames to make an animation myself.

Got one

Looking back to my complete failure, I decided to download a dog and animate it. It was cool, I programed big part of the prototype with this one. It just run so I felt there was something missing, and I was running out of time. I found a fox with all the animations I needed and more.

Changed to fox

I finally found a fox with all the animations I needed to make the game feel good. It jumped and turned left and right just as I wanted. I added a capsule collider and it worked perfect.

BlendTrees

The Blend Tree management was a challenge that I had to face. Althoug the model and animations for the fox were already created, a substancial amount of research was necessary to real Unity's animator tool.

Basic UI

This one-month journey was to be over. One of my partners did these sprites in photoshop and I integrated them to the project. from what I learned with the UI I build the Distance and gold system and the time arrived. I missed the health of the player which was the most important mechanic for the prototype. At the end the prototype was useless, not playable at all but this story led me to my next project.
